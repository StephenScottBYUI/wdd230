Hick's Law - Google

Google.com

The landing page of Google.com is a perfect example of Hick's Law. Hick's Law is the concept that the more choices there are on the page the longer it will take for the viewer to come to a decision. Google does a great job at limiting the choices available on the landing page. There is the main item which is the search bar and a few other options. This moves you right along to the main purpose of the website, your search results.

Fitt's Law - Dropbox

Dropbox.com

Fitt’s Law explains the relationship between the size of the target, the distance to the target, and the time it takes to press the target. Basically, this means that user design must take the button size and crowding around clickable areas into consideration. An excellent example of this is Dropbox’s Contact sales button. It is big and easy to click, and the design of the button draws your attention to it right away.

Visual Heirarchy - Typeform

Typeform.com

The website Typeform.com uses visual hierarchy to grab the attention of the viewer and easily convey the purpose of the website. Visual hierarchy is using different visuals to direct your eye accross the screen. Looking at the screenshot above your sight is immediately drawn to the heading. This is because of both the font size and the white space around the heading. After seeing the headline, you are lured to the animation that is taking place in the purple box. This animation shows examples of the forms that you can use. Then immediately after you are directed to the call-to-action box to “get started – it’s free”.
